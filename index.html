I

The goal here isn't effort — it's reopening range that's been sitting dormant. Think of it as physical archaeology: you're not building something new, you're uncovering what's already there. Never force range. Ease in, breathe, and let the stretch happen.

The Four Drills
01
10 reps  ·  slow

Cobra to
Downward Dog

Lie face-down, hands flat directly under your shoulders — not beside your head. Press up slowly into cobra: pelvis stays on the floor, elbows soft, chest lifts. Hold 2 seconds and feel the thoracic extension, not just your lower back arching. Then tuck toes, push hips up and back into downward dog — heels reaching toward the floor. Hold 2 seconds. The transition between the two positions is the work. Do it in slow motion and you'll feel exactly where you're restricted.

On the board: The cobra position is the chest-press phase of your pop-up. If your thoracic spine is locked up, your hands never fully leave the board — they drag, which kills the timing of everything downstream.

02
60 sec  ·  each side

Hip Flexor
Lunge Stretch

Step one foot forward into a deep lunge, rear knee on the ground — use a folded towel if needed. Think about sinking the hips forward and down, not just bending the front knee. The stretch should be felt deep in the front of the rear hip. Raise arms overhead to deepen it. Hold 60 seconds each side — don't shortchange it, this tissue is dense and needs time. Breathe into the stretch throughout.

On the board: When your front foot lands in the pop-up, your hip flexors must be long enough to allow your hips to drop low and forward into your stance. Short hip flexors put your weight in the back seat — every time.

03
2 min  ·  daily

Ankle Circles &
Dorsiflexion

Sit on the floor, legs extended. For circles: slow, full-range rotations — 10 each direction per ankle. For dorsiflexion: loop a towel around the ball of your foot, pull toes toward your shin, hold 3–4 seconds, release. Wall version: face a wall, toes 2–3 inches from the baseboard, try to touch your knee to the wall without your heel lifting. That gap tells you exactly where you are.

On the board: When you land your front foot in the pop-up, your ankle must dorsiflex to get weight forward over the board. Stiff ankles push your weight back. Subtle — but it shows up on every single wave.

04
5 min  ·  eyes closed

Pop-Up
Visualization

Lie on your back, close your eyes, and mentally run through the full motion in real time at pop-up speed: feel the water under the board, hear the wave, feel your hands press, hips drive, front foot plant, hands release, eyes come up to the horizon. Run it 4–5 times per session. For it to work, it must be specific and sensory — not abstract. If you find yourself losing focus or rushing, slow down and rebuild the sensation from the beginning.

The science: The brain fires the same motor patterns during vivid visualization as during physical execution. You already know this movement. These are free reps — and your nervous system can't tell the difference when done with full sensory detail.
